Deliver Us

By cj
December 19, 2020

‘Deliver us from evil’ - Matt 6:13

These words are from the prayer framework that the Lord Jesus taught His disciples when they asked Him to teach them how to pray. Here's looking at two key words, 'Deliver us.'

First, the word ‘deliver’. Someone prays for deliverance, only when he or she is bound or imprisoned or tied up. Thousands across the world are in prison today for crimes committed or even crimes uncommitted, and they pray and groan for deliverance from their bars and chains. To even begin praying this way, we need to recognize and acknowledge those aspects of our lives that are yet in bondage.

Though Christ offers all his disciples the great and inexpressible gift of salvation, deliverance from the terrible grip of evil and its harmful effects is something that one realizes only in gradual stages. Though deliverance in total is guaranteed, and we will truly enjoy it in completeness one day, yet while we are on this planet in our mortal frames we need to work out our individual experiences of salvation day in and day out. Moreover, and more importantly, we need divine help. This word deliver is a recognition of that truth. When I request a higher power for deliverance, I express my understanding of my own weakness. I bring to the forefront the knowledge of human helplessness and powerlessness to withstand temptation. But this word also expresses faith. The faith that the Father is powerful to save and deliver all His children.

Second, the word ‘us’. I am not only to pray for myself but for others. In the area of temptation, my brother’s spiritual condition should be my concern, as much as my own is.

May these two words find daily mention in our daily prayers for ourselves and for our fellow pilgrims.
