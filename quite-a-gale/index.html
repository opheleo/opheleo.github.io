Quite a Gale

By cj
May 28, 2020

Lightning striking those tired eyes,
Staring dully at the slow heavy clouds,
Hanging dark above the fearful fields,
Once green now gray in the fading light,

Sparking distress in pain soaked hearts,
Spongefuls of dark melancholy tragedy,
The rains falling steadily without respite,
Pretending to appear but hides again,

Hope wilting under the onslaught,
Petals and leaves burdened by the weight,
A thundering waterfall on delicate things,
Made for better times and fairer winds.
