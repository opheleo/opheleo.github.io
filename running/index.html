Running

Now flee from youthful lusts and pursue righteousness, faith, love and peace, with those who call on the Lord from a pure heart. - 2 Timothy 2:22

There are three running aspects to the above verse: running from, running towards, and running with.

We must run away from youthful lusts. What are these? Are they lusts of the flesh indulged only in youth? Or can they also be lusts commonly begun to be indulged in by those who are youthful but often carry over well into adulthood? A youthful person lives in a state of semi-blindness indulging in dangerous and thoughtless behaviour not realizing the fleeting nature of youth, and closing his eyes to the consequences and risks of ones actions. The harmful consequences of our youthful lusts build over time leading us ever closer to eventual spiritual and sometimes physical death. As adults are we still indulging in youthful lusts? May we run from these even as this verse exhorts us.

What are we to run towards? Righteousness, faith, love, and peace. Do we even attempt righteous acts of service? Do we dare dream in faith to believe and expect great things from God? Do we love selflessly? Do we pursue a life of peace and tranquillity or are we always desiring the chaos and stimulation of the world’s buzz?

Lastly, who must we run with? Only with those who call on the Lord from a pure heart. How do we identify these so we can run with them? Only when we seek fellow runners will we find them. And we will seek only if we realise that we cannot run alone. May we break out of our self-contained bubbles and find, encourage, and inspire our fellow runners, and help one another to run another day.

God bless you with a wonderful run today!
