Why focus on doom and gloom?

By cj
April 20, 2021

God has given us prophetic revelations in His Word not that we can stay ahead of the curve. That is practically impossible. The things that are bound to happen will happen, and we can do nothing to prevent them from eventually happening, though we may have a role in speeding up or slowing these circumstances down a little.

But when we see that the things that were written ages ago have been true of past events, and are inexorably coming true even in the present, this ought to make us realize the truth of His Word and that it never fails, even though we may misinterpret or misread it at times. This also ought to make us realize that the other things said in the Word that haven't come true yet are quite possibly and very probably going to come true as well. Thus the gradual unfolding of the prophetic revelations are but the tokens of their truthfulness and the tokens to God’s omniscience.

Also, prophecy, though seemingly negative is actually positive, because along with the doom and gloom it also presents a living hope for the future. This hope is a means of escape, it foretells of a glorious incorruptible future, beyond the present mess and muck. To drag humanity’s attention away from this muck we are mired in and point us towards this hope of salvation, is the point of prophecy that really matters.

Though seemingly implausible, concepts such as hell, eternity, heaven and so on thus take on a new meaning and urgency in the light of prophetic truths. If what is prophetic has been eerily true in the past, and if we roundly ignore what is foretold of the future, we do so at our own peril. Thus, spending time understanding the truths and trends of prophetic import and their implications for humanity is an endeavour worth pursuing.
